RISE & Luxembourg Red Cross:

TRANSFORMING DISASTER RESPONSE IN MADAGASCAR

From 2023 to 2025, WASDI participated in the Humanitarian Innovation Accelerator programme, supported by the WFP Innovation Accelerator, the Government of the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg, and the Austrian Development Cooperation (ADC). The programme supports innovative solutions to address urgent challenges facing vulnerable populations and humanitarian actors.

In collaboration with the Luxembourg Institute of Science and Technology (LIST) and the Luxembourg Red Cross (AICRL), WASDI developed RISE and tested its effectiveness in real-world disaster response. Partnering with the Malagasy Red Cross Society (CRM), the team chose Madagascar, a country frequently hit by cyclones, floods, and droughts, as the testing ground.

The project focused on Maroantsetra, a region devastated by Cyclone Cheneso in 2023, to evaluate how RISE could improve rapid needs assessments compared to traditional methods.

Collaborative Innovation

WASDI built the technical core of RISE, while LIST provided scientific expertise, including developing drought and settlement monitoring tools. AICRL led real-world validation, ensuring RISE met disaster response requirements through field testing and practical guidance.

User-Led Refinement

RISE evolved through iterative testing, focus groups, and training with AICRL and Malagasy Red Cross (CRM) teams. This process ensured the platform was user-friendly, efficient, and fully aligned with humanitarian operations.

Field Validation Success

During a head-to-head test during Cyclone Cheneso, two teams, one using traditional methods (field visits, interviews, and secondary data) and the other using RISE, assessed flood impact, population needs, and infrastructure damage. The exercise demonstrated RISE’s speed, accuracy, cost-efficiency, and safety in crisis response.

What’s next for RISE?

Building on the success in Madagascar, the RISE team is expanding its footprint to support global humanitarian operations. By continuously integrating new satellite data sources and AI-driven analytics, RISE is evolving to predict risks before they occur, allowing teams to stay one step ahead of disasters.
