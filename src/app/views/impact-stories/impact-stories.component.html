RISE & Luxembourg Red Cross:

TRANSFORMING DISASTER RESPONSE IN MADAGASCAR

From 2023 to 2025, WASDI participated in the Humanitarian Innovation Accelerator Programme, backed by the WFP Innovation Accelerator, the Government of the Grand-Duchy of Luxembourg, and the Austrian Development Cooperation (ADC). The program supports innovative solutions to address the urgent challenges faced by vulnerable populations and humanitarian actors.

In collaboration with the Luxembourg Institute of Science and Technology (LIST) and the Luxembourg Red Cross (AICRL), WASDI developed RISE and tested its effectiveness in real-world disaster response. Partnering with the Malagasy Red Cross Society (CRM), the team chose Madagascar, a country frequently hit by cyclones, floods, and droughts, as the testing ground.

The project focused on Maroantsetra, a region devastated by Cyclone Cheneso in 2023, to evaluate how RISE could enhance rapid needs assessments compared to traditional methods.

Project Highlights

TRANSFORMING DISASTER RESPONSE IN MADAGASCAR

Collaborative Innovation

WASDI built the technical core of RISE, while LIST provided scientific expertise — including the development of drought and settlement monitoring tools. AICRL led real-world validation, ensuring RISE met the demands of disaster response through field testing and practical guidance.

User-Driven Refinement

RISE evolved through iterative testing, focus groups, and training with AICRL and Malagasy Red Cross (CRM) teams. This process guaranteed the platform was user-friendly, efficient, and fully aligned with humanitarian operations.

Field Validation Success

In a head-to-head test during Cyclone Cheneso, two teams — one using traditional methods (field visits, interviews, and secondary data) and the other using RISE — assessed flood impact, population needs, and infrastructure damage.
